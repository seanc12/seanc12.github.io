Each year 96.3 The Blaze airs the Top 96 requested songs of the year.

With that said, here was your 96.3 The Blaze Top 96 of 2015

32. Saint Asonia Better Place

"Better Place" is the first single from the debut self-titled studio album, Saint Asonia by alternative metal band Saint Asonia which was released on May 16, 2015. Also the song has been released digitally via Spotify and iTunes.

31. Five Finger Death Punch Wash It All Away

"Wash It All Away" is a song by heavy metal band Five Finger Death Punch, from their sixth studio album Got Your Six. It was released in October 2015 as the second single from the album.

30. Seether Nobody Praying For Me

"Nobody Praying for Me" is a song by South African rock band Seether. It was released on 28 April 2015 as the third single from their sixth studio album Isolate and Medicate.

29. Bring Me The Horizon Throne

Remember the moment you left me alone and Broke every promise you ever made I was an ocean, lost in.

28. Slipknot The Devil In I

"The Devil in I" is a single for American heavy metal band Slipknot's fifth album, .5: The Gray Chapter. It is the second single for the album and the first to be released to radio and music channels, with the previous single "The Negative One" being a digital only single.

27. Chris Cornell Nearly Forgot My Broken Heart

"Nearly Forgot My Broken Heart" is a song by American rock musician Chris Cornell. It was released as the lead single from his fourth studio album Higher Truth (2015). The song hit the top 5 on Billboard's Mainstream Rock chart, and is also his first entry on that chart since 2007's "No Such Thing", as well as his first entry on the Alternative Songs chart since 1999's "Can't Change Me" and his first song to enter the Adult Alternative Songs chart.

26. Godsmack Something Different

"Something Different" is a song by the rock band Godsmack. It served as the second single from the band's sixth studio album 1000hp. The song was released in September 2014.

25. Bring Me The Horizon Drown

"Drown" is a song by the British rock band Bring Me the Horizon on October 21, 2014. Originally released as a stand alone single, a re-recorded version features on the band's fifth studio album That's the Spirit. The track, musically, marks a shift away from the band's previously established metalcore sound. Rather, it was described by critics as other types of rock, including alternative rock, emo, and pop punk, also being described as "arena rock fire".

24. Seether Words As Weapons

"Words as Weapons" is a song by South African post-grunge/alternative metal band Seether. It was released on 1 May 2014, as the lead single from their sixth studio album Isolate and Medicate.

23. Three Days Grace Human Race

Three Days Grace are currently rocking a thinker of a track in their latest single “Human Race,” and now they’ve given us a brand new video that’s also pretty thought provoking.

22. Papa Roach with Maria Brink Gravity

In This Moment’s Maria Brink appears on Papa Roach’s current single “Gravity,” and she joined Jacoby Shaddix and crew onstage in Moline, Ill., for the live debut of the track.

21. Chris Cornell Heavy Is The Head

The group joins Bon Jovi as the only act to have topped Country Airplay and Mainstream Rock Songs.

20. Red Sun Rising The Otherside

Red Sun Rising has teamed up with Rock on The Range to premiere their brand new song “The Otherside.”

19. All That Remains For You

You need to figure out the things that plague you here at home.

18. Nothing More Jenny

"Jenny" is a song from American rock band Nothing More. As the third single from their self titled album, the song discusses the struggles with mental illness that Jenna, the sister of Nothing More lead vocalist Jonny Hawkins, has.

17. Halestorm Apocalyptic

"Apocalyptic" is a song by the American hard rock band Halestorm. It was released on January 12, 2015, as the lead single from the band's third studio album, Into the Wild Life. The video for the song was released on January 28.

16. Breaking Benjamin Angels Fall

"Angels Fall" is a song by American rock band Breaking Benjamin. Released soon after the album's lead single "Failure" on April 14, 2015,[1] the track is the second single on their fifth studio album Dark Before Dawn and is the album's third track overall.

15. Papa Roach Face Everything And Rise

"Face Everything and Rise" is a song by American rock band Papa Roach. It served as the lead single from the band's seventh studio album F.E.A.R.. The song was released on November 4, 2014, and reached #1 on the Mainstream Rock Tracks.

14. Disturbed The Vengeful One

"The Vengeful One" is a song by heavy metal band Disturbed, from their sixth album Immortalized. It was released on June 23, 2015, and is the band's first released song in 4 years, since the release of The Lost Children.

13. Foo Fighters Congregation

"Congregation" is a song by American rock band Foo Fighters. It is the third official single and the second radio single from their eighth studio album Sonic Highways. It was released on October 31, 2014. The song features country music singer Zac Brown, with whom Grohl collaborated with on Zac Brown Band's extended play The Grohl Sessions, Vol. 1.

12. Halestorm I Am The Fire

Simply put, it’s hard to find a hotter band in rock these days than Halestorm and the heat just got a little warmer for the band with the debut of their new video for “I Am the Fire.”

11. Slash Bent To Fly

"Bent to Fly" is a song by American hard rock guitarist Slash, featuring vocalist Myles Kennedy and backing band The Conspirators. Written by Slash and Kennedy, it was released as the second single from the guitarist's third solo album (the second with Kennedy and The Conspirators) World on Fire.

10. Seether Same Damn Life

"Same Damn Life" is a song by South African rock band Seether. It was released on 16 September 2014 as the second single from their sixth studio album Isolate and Medicate.

9. Theory Of A Deadman Angel

Angel is the third single on Theory of a Deadman's fifth studio album Savages. The single was released on February 24, 2015.

8. Foo Fighters Something From Nothing

"Something from Nothing" is a song by the American rock band Foo Fighters from their eighth studio album Sonic Highways. It was released as the album's lead single on October 16, 2014. Recorded at Steve Albini's Electrical Audio studio, the song was influenced by the Chicago music scene.

7. Shinedown Cut The Cord

"Cut the Cord" is a song by American hard rock band Shinedown. It was released on June 29, 2015 as the lead single from the band's fifth studio album, Threat to Survival. The song reached No. 1 on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart, their ninth single to do so. It was featured as the official theme song of Hell in a Cell (2015).

6. Pop Evil Footsteps

"Footsteps" is the lead single by American rock band Pop Evil from Up, their fourth studio album.

5. Three Days Grace I Am Machine

"I Am Machine" is a song by Canadian rock band Three Days Grace. The song was released on September 30, 2014.[1]It is the second single off the band's fifth studio album Human.

4. Breaking Benjamin Failure

"Failure" is a song by American rock band Breaking Benjamin. The song was released on March 23, 2015 as the lead single on the band's fifth studio album, Dark Before Dawn. The track marks the band's first release since the start of an extended hiatus entered upon in mid-2010, and is also the first recording to feature a different lineup alongside singer, writer, and guitarist Benjamin Burnley. The track is their third single to reach No. 1 on the Mainstream Rock Tracks chart, after "Breath" (2006) and "I Will Not Bow" (2009), and spent nine weeks there.

3. Halestorm Amen

Amen is the second single on Halestorm's third studio album Into the Wild Life.

2. Five Finger Death Punch Jekyll And Hyde

"Jekyll and Hyde" is a single by American heavy metal band Five Finger Death Punch from their sixth studio album, Got Your Six. It is the first single from the album, and is the twentieth single overall from the band, which was released on 15 June 2015.

1. Royal Blood Little Monster

"Little Monster" is a song written by Mike Kerr and Ben Thatcher of British rock duo Royal Blood. The song was originally recorded by the duo for release as a single, appearing on the band's second single of the same name, released by Black Mammoth Records and Warner Bros. Records on 10 February 2014. The single marked the band's first release under Warner Bros. Records after signing with the label in 2013. The track later appeared as the second track on the band's debut extended play Out of the Black, and as the sixth track on the band's eponymous debut studio album, Royal Blood. It is currently the duo's highest charting single.
